Virtual Environment Navigation Laboratory (VENLab)

Location Brown University,
Providence, RI
Employment Dates March 2006 - May 2011
Contact VENLabCrew@gmail.com
Website VENLab Site

The VENLab is a research facility designed to study problems related to perception and action, with an emphasis on the visual control of locomotion and navigation. This work has a wide range of applications including robotics, computer animation, architecture and the design of navigation tools like GPS.

I first joined the VENLab in the Spring of my Freshman year at Brown as a Research Assistant. Initially, my role was concentrated on helping run participants through studies conducted in virtual reality, however, I soon began to take a role in the coding and development of the VR experiments becoming the lab's first in-house developer. Over the next five years I oversaw the expansion of the dev team to four people, as we built two seperate frameworks (one for each of the research focus areas of the lab, Navigation and Locomotion).

Researcher - Small Arms Survey Dates: June 2007 - Auguest 2007
Location: Old Dominion University, Norfolk, VA
Website: Small Arms Survey

The Small Arms Survey serves as the principal international source of public information on all aspects of small arms and armed violence and as a resource for governments, policy-makers, researchers, and activists.

My role in the project revolved around collecting small arms data for both Latin American and post-Communist countries under the direction of Professor Aaron Karp. In the process I also worked to improve the algortims for extrapolting the total small arms stockpiles for these countries, through refinement of the parameters regarding military foce size and size/scope of active military conflicts.

Teacher - Learning Bridge (now Breakthrough Collaborative) Dates: Summers 2004, 2005, and 2006
Location: Norfolk, VA
Website: Breakthrough at Norfolk Academy

Breakthrough at Norfolk Academy is a tuition-free, academic enrichment program designed to prepare motivated Norfolk public middle school students for success in college-preparatory high school programs and to provide talented high school and college students with hands-on teaching experience that will inspire them to pursue careers in education. The program consists of a full school day for six weeks in the summer. Students take literature, language arts, history, math, and science classes in addition to electives as diverse as foreign language, photography, dance, and journalism.
